Family Leporidae

A brief look at Hares and Rabbits, aka Bunnies

Leporidae,latin for"those that resemble lepus- the hare" is the family name for rabbits and hares. It contains
over 70 species. They have short, furry tails and elongated ears and hind legs (which make them distinctly cute).

They are commonly referred to as "rabbit" collectively and with "bunny" being another common name for the family.
Many countries all over the world , with the exception of Antarctica and Australia, have indigenous species
of Leporidae. Of note, the European rabbit, Oryctolagus cuniculus, has been introduced to most of
Oceania and to many other islands, where they unfortunately pose a serious ecological and commercial threat.
But that's a discussion for another day.

Some Bunny Facts

  • A single bunny is a lonely bunny; they are very social and happiest in the company of other bunnies.
    They can become extremely sad and depressed if kept on their own. Please consider getting them in pairs
  • Domesticated bunnies can live between 8 to 12 years.
  • Most bunnies can turn their ears 180° allowing them to pinpoint the exact location of a sound.
    Their eyesight is really good too; they have almost 360° vision.
  • Baby bunnies are called "kittens", female rabbits are called "does" and male bunnies are called "bucks".
  • Bunnies are crepuscular which means they're mostly active at dusk and dawn and certain periods during the
    night.At other times, they love a good snooze.
